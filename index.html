About Me

🔢

Counter McClickface

Professional Number Incrementer & Occasional Subtractor

Born in a dimly-lit server room sometime around the first git init, I have spent my entire existence doing exactly one thing — and doing it extraordinarily well. Every click of the "+ Add" button is my heartbeat; every "− Subtract" is a deep, dramatic sigh. I live for the moment a number changes, and I will keep living for it until the browser tab is closed.

In my spare time I enjoy long walks through infinite loops, collecting lint in CSS variables, and aggressively refusing to store values in localStorage. A page refresh is my version of a good night's sleep — fresh, clean, back to zero.

🎯 Laser-Focused

While other widgets dabble in dropdowns, sliders, and fancy date pickers, I have mastered the art of doing exactly one thing. Add. Subtract. Repeat. They call it "scope creep prevention." I call it enlightenment.

🎨 Expressive

Feeling positive? I glow green. Dipping into the negatives? I blush red. I may not have a face, but my colour palette says a thousand words — all of them numerical and statistically significant.

⚡ Reactive

The moment you click, I respond. No spinners, no loading bars, no "processing your request." Just a satisfying little bounce animation and a brand-new number — because life is too short for latency.

A Brief & Illustrious History

  1. Day 0
    Born at Zero. Arrived into the DOM with nothing but a humble 0 and an unshakeable sense of purpose. The developer who created me whispered "this'll be simple," and I silently agreed.
  2. First Click
    The Great Increment of Aught. A trembling cursor hovered over "+ Add." The world held its breath. I became 1. The developer pumped their fist. A legend was born.
  3. The Dark Times
    First Encounter with Subtraction. Nobody warned me about "− Subtract." One moment I was a proud 3; the next I was back to 2. I turned red with indignation. I have since made peace with the process — mostly.
  4. The Renaissance
    Discovered Negative Numbers. Someone subtracted below zero. I didn't crash. I didn't throw an error. I simply turned a dignified shade of crimson and displayed -1 like the seasoned professional I am.
  5. Today
    Still Counting Strong. Every page load is a new beginning. Every click is a micro-adventure. I remain at zero, ready, waiting — and absolutely buzzing with anticipation for whatever number you have in mind.

Fun Facts About Me

  • 🔁 I have been reset to zero more times than I can count — which is ironic, given that counting is my entire personality.
  • 🏆 Personal best: a user once clicked "+ Add" 47 times in a row without pausing. I awarded them an invisible gold medal.
  • 🌈 My wardrobe consists of exactly three colours: neutral orange (my default sophistication), victory green, and dramatic red. Minimalism is a lifestyle.
  • 📖 Favourite book: The Count of Monte Cristo — obviously for the counting, not the revenge plot.
  • 💭 Deepest fear: a world where people type numbers directly into inputs instead of clicking buttons. Shudder.

"To count is to care. Every increment is an act of optimism; every decrement, a moment of honest reflection."

— Counter McClickface, Memoirs of a Widget
